Affordance-aware Interactive Decision-making and Environment-grounded Planning for Ambiguous Tasks

Paper Video Code arXiv

Abstract

This study aims to address the challenge of enabling robots to perform interactive exploration and exploitation efficiently when faced with ambiguous human instructions in unfamiliar environments. Faced with ambiguous instructions like “I am thirsty”, existing methods based on vision-language models (VLMs) frequently fail to initiate or sustain a series of interactions to search for relevant items—such as drinks or cups, due to their limited environmental knowledge, dependence on externally provided interactions, and inefficient reasoning, which impede their ability to parse and respond to ambiguous instructions in real time. To tackle this challenge, we introduce the Affordance-Aware Interactive DecisionMaking and Environment-Grounded Planning for Ambiguous Tasks (AIDE), an innovative hybrid online-offline planning framework that integrates interactive exploration with visual-language reasoning. AIDE employs VLMs to generate executable steps, encompassing affordance reasoning and tool operation guidance, thereby facilitating zero-shot understanding of ambiguous instructions through interactive scene exploration. Moreover, AIDE expands the sample data from online planning to create a library, comprising over 1,000 task samples, thereby facilitating efficient offline planning while maintaining real-time responsiveness. Comprehensive experiments conducted in both simulation and physical settings demonstrate that AIDE achieves an accuracy rate exceeding 70% in task planning. And particularly in physical experiments, AIDE achieves over 95% accuracy in the closed-loop continuous decision-making process. Moreover, the offline method supports closed-loop decision-making at frequencies of up to 10 Hz. These results highlight its superior performance compared to existing VLM-based planners in diverse open-world scenarios.

AIDE Method

Framework of the method: Given an ambiguous instruction and corresponding surrounding environment, the AIDE offline framework makes good use of the task planning data generated by the AIDE online framework, and relies on the affordance-based retrieval library to achieve efficient real-time task planning. The designed visible and invisible interactive exploration strategies ensure the closed-loop and robustness of the AIDE method.
