AIDE: Affordance-aware Interactive Decision-making and Environment-grounded Planning for Ambiguous Tasks

Paper Video Code arXiv

Abstract

This research tackles the challenge of enabling robots to efficiently perform interactive exploration and closedloop planning when faced with ambiguous human instructions in unfamiliar environments. Existing Visual Language Model (VLM)-based approaches often fall short due to limited environmental knowledge and dependence on externally provided interactions, hindering their ability to interpret and respond to vague instructions such as facing task“I’m really thirsty”, the robot needs explor weither it on a table or in a fridge in truly environment. To address this, we propose AIDE (Affordanceaware Interactive Decision-making and Environment-grounded Planning for Ambiguous Tasks), a novel online-offline planning framework that integrates interactive exploration with visuallanguage reasoning. AIDE employs VLMs to generate executable steps, including affordance reasoning and tool operation guidance, etc. It supports zero-shot understanding of ambiguous commands through scene interaction. It further augments sample data from online planning to construct a library of over 1,000 task samples, enabling efficient offline planning with realtime responsiveness. Extensive experiments in both simulation and physical robot settings show that AIDE achieves over 70% accuracy in task execution and supports closed-loop decisionmaking at 10 Hz, outperforming existing VLM-based planners in diverse open-world scenarios.

AIDE Method

Overview of the method: Given a task instruction and corresponding surrounding environment, the offline framework makes good use of the task planning data generated by the online framework, and relies on the affordance scoring and multimodal similarity matching to achieve efficient real-time task planning. And the designed visible exploration and invisible exploration strategies ensure the closed-loop and robustness of the AIDE method.
